Who Am I?

About Me

I am currently an Associate Professor in Computer Science at the University of Strasbourg.
I am conducting my research in the Data Science and Knowledge team (SDC) of the engineering sciences, computer science, and imaging laboratory (ICube), since 09/2023.
I teach in the Computer Science Department of the IUT Robert Schuman.

Previously, I have been:

  • Associate Professor in the CSTB team (09/2021 -- 08/2023)
  • Postdoctoral researcher in the Discrete Event Models Formalisms team (MOFED) of the Computer Science & Systems Laboratory (LIS) at the University of Aix-Marseille (09/2020 -- 08/2021).
  • Postdoctoral researcher in the Sustainable Environment team (ED) of the Research and Teaching Center of Environmental Geosciences (CEREGE) at the University of Aix-Marseille, in collaboration with the Center for Environmental Implications of Nanotechnology (CEINT) at Duke University (09/2019 -- 08/2020).
  • Teaching and Research Assistant (ATER) at the UFR of Mathematics and Computer Science of the University of Strasbourg (09/2017 -- 08/2019).
  • PhD student at the Data Science and Knowledge team (SDC) of the ICube laboratory at the University of Strasbourg (05/2015 -- 09/2018).
What's my interest?

Research Topics

The research topics I am currently working on, or have recently worked on, include:

  • Multimodal LLMs guided by knowledge: integrating structured knowledge (ontologies, knowledge graphs, rules) with vision, text, time-series and signals to improve grounding, reasoning, retrieval-augmented generation, safety, and explainability.
  • Neuro-symbolic AI and XAI: exploring hybrid AI models that combine symbolic reasoning with machine learning to enhance interpretability and explainability.
  • Knowledge-centric reasoning (ontologies, rules & KGs): model complex concepts and relations, encode expert rules, and support inference & qualitative reasoning to derive and validate new knowledge.
  • Discrete event simulation: using simulation models to represent and analyze the behavior of systems (neural networks) over time through discrete events.

These research areas have been applied in various fields, including Industry 4.0 and Medical domains, improving automation, diagnostics, and decision-making.
