Who Am I?

About Me

I'm a postdoc researcher at Sustainable Environment Group in the Centre for Research and Teaching in Environmental Geoscience (CEREGE), a joint research centre (UM 34), incorporating Aix-Marseille University (AMU), the CNRS (UMR7330), the IRD (UMR 161), the COLLEGE DE France (CDF), and the INRA.
After getting my scientific baccalaureate in 2008, I obtained a fundamental bachelor's degree in computer science in 2012. I hold a Master's degree in Data, Knowledge, and Distributed Systems in 2014 at the Faculty of Law, Economic, and Management Sciences - University of Jendouba - Tunisia as well.

In September 2018, I have obtained a Ph.D. in computer science with my thesis “ Semantic approaches for the meta-optimization of complex biomolecular networks” within the Data Science and Knowledge Group (SDC) in collaboration with the Complex Systems, Translational Bioinformatics (CSTB) team of ICube laboratory in the University of Strasbourg, France - under the supervision of Prof. Cecilia Zanni-Merk.

The subject of my thesis focus on the « transittability » of complex biomolecular networks. This concept expresses the idea of steering a biomolecular network from an undesired condition (generally associated with a disease) to a desired, hopefully healthy state. One of the main contributions of this thesis is an intelligent system that enables biologists to simulate the state changes of biomolecular networks with the goal of steering their behaviours. This prototype is based in the online Biomolecular Network ontology (BNO), an ontology that we develop to formalize the domain knowledge of complex biomolecular networks making it visible and accessible to all biologists working on this topic.

What's my interest?

Research Topics

Research topics that I'm working on, or have recently worked on, include:

Knowledge Engineering: My main research interests are focused in Knowledge Engineering, Ontological Engineering and Semantic Web. More precisely, I am interested in the areas of Knowledge Modelling (including conceptualization, formalization and implementation), Ontology Development, Ontology Evaluation, and Ontology Design Patterns.

Modeling and Simulation: In this area, my research focus on a collection of formal systems such, first-order logic, formal logic, and reasoning modeling. I am working on how to use these mathematical models for the repr representing the dynamics of the system via simulation, and exploring system behavior in a formulated way which is often either not possible, or too risky in the real world.

Optimization of Complex Systems: In this area, I am focusing on discrete optimization methods (exact, heuristic and meta-heuristic methods) as well as hybrid approaches by coupling different methods: simulation and optimization, multi-criteria decision and multi-objective optimization, exact and approximate methods (meta-heuristics) with the aim of solving large and complex problems.

My application domains are Systems biology and Nanotechnologies.
